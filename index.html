About Me

I started my career as a Geomatics Engineer and later earned my Master's in Remote Sensing. For me, the core focus has always been understanding the physical world from a spatial perspective. I have also participated in geophysics projects, taking responsibility for 2D/3D cartography processing, ArcGIS terrain analysis, and interpreting geospatial data for critical technical decisions. Recently, I decided to push my fundamental understanding further by taking on a Diplomado in Modern Physics; diving into quantum mechanics and thermodynamics is a new challenge, but I believe that a solid grasp of physical laws makes me a better spatial scientist.

Over the years, as the volume of geospatial data exploded, I realized traditional analysis wasn't enough. That's why I transitioned deeply into Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision through intense personal study and independent project development. Instead of manually interpreting large volumes of data from diverse sources, I learned how to build custom neural networks and detection models to do the heavy lifting, turning massive datasets into precise, actionable insights.

However, a great scientific model isn't very helpful if it's stuck on a local machine. That realization led me to Full-Stack software engineering. Working with frameworks like Spring Boot, Node.js, and React allows me to bridge the gap between science and software.
